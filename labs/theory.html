Theory

Communication/transmission of data from sender to receiver takes place in a computer network.
Analog/digital data traverses through communication media from source to destination in form of signals.
The transmitting signals may undergo attenuation and distortion as they travel over large distances.
Therefore there is a need to match the properties of transmitted signal as per the communication media
for which digital data can be converted into digital signals.

Line encoding: The process of converting digital data into digital signals is referred as Line Encoding.
With the help of line encoding schemes, a sequence of bits are converted into digital signal(encoding)
which then get converted back into bits at the receiver end (decoding).

  1. RZ-Unipolar Encoding Scheme: RZ uses three levels of amplitude i.e. positive, negative, zero. it returns to zero in the middle of each bit. 1 : positiveto-zero, 0 : negative-to-zero.
  2. NRZ-L Encoding Scheme: In NRZ-L type of encoding, high volt is assigned for logical 0 bit and low volt is assigned for logical 1. Level of voltage determines bit value.
  3. NRZ-I Encoding Scheme: For logical 1 the signal will be inverted, but when it encounters logical 0 then signal will not undergo any transition.
  4. Manchester Encoding Scheme: A logic 0 is indicated by a 0 to 1 transition at the centre of the bit and logic 1 by 1 to 0 transition.
  5. Differential Manchester Encoding Scheme: It combines the idea of RZ and NRZ-I.If the next bit is 0,transition will occur but if the next bit is 1 , no transition will occur.
  6. AMI Encoding Scheme: In Alternate Mark Inversion or AMI, 0 bit is represented by Zero level and binary 1 is represented by alternating positive and negative voltages.
  7. Pseudoternary Encoding Scheme: Pseudoternary is similar to AMI with slight difference that alternating positive and negative is used for binary 0 instead of binary 1.
