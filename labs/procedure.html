Procedure

  1. RZ-Unipolar Encoding Scheme: RZ uses three levels of amplitude i.e. positive, negative, zero. it returns to zero in the middle of each bit. 1 : positiveto-zero, 0 : negative-to-zero. You can refer to the figure below for a better understanding of the encoding scheme.
  2. NRZ-L Encoding Scheme: For logical 1 the signal will be inverted, but when it encounters logical 0 then signal will not undergo any transition. This is the NRZ-Invert.
  3. NRZ-I Encoding Scheme: For logical 1 the signal will be inverted, but when it encounters logical 0 then signal will not undergo any transition. This is the NRZ-Invert.
  4. Manchester Encoding Scheme: It is a combination of NRZ-L and RZ. The duration of each bit is divided into two halves. 0 is and 1 is .
  5. Differential Manchester Encoding Scheme: It combines the idea of RZ and NRZ-I.If the next bit is 0,transition will occur but if the next bit is 1 , no transition will occur.
  6. AMI Encoding Scheme: In Alternate Mark Inversion or AMI, 0 bit is represented by Zero level and binary 1 is represented by alternating positive and negative voltages.
  7. Pseudoternary Encoding Scheme: Pseudoternary is similar to AMI with slight difference that alternating positive and negative is used for binary 0 instead of binary 1.
